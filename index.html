Exam period is 2 weeks in January and potentially 5 weeks starting in May Provisional module marks for Autumn Semester usually available at end of Feb. Confirmed at June Examination board which considers all marks for the academic year. Resit examinations normally held in August and considered by the Examination Board in September. A doctors note is essential if you miss an exam due to illness.

CC BY 2.0 by Xavi

Need to achieve all 120 credits in the taught part before proceeding to the next stage. Pass mark for each module is 50%. If you resit and pass a module you will be credited the minimum pass mark for that module of 50%. You only have one attempt to resit a failed module. If you are doing a “with Placement” variant of your programme then you will need pass all your modules and obtain a suitable placement to proceed to the Placement stage. The Pass mark for the placement stage is 50%. The Dissertation stage follows the taught stage for one year Masters and the placement stage for two-year Masters. Pass mark for Dissertation stage is 50%

CC BY NC 2.0 by Daniel Parks

Your worked will be assessed at Master’s level. Criteria may be different than what you are used to: If you are achieving marks in the 50s you are showing you can work at the level expected of a Master’s level ent If your marks are in the 60s this is showing good work at Master’s level If your marks are in the 70s then you are showing very good work at Master’s level. A mark of 80 and above usually indicates excellence at Master’s level and for many modules is only awarded for exceptional work.

CC BY NC ND 2.0 by Chris Guy

If you perform very well throughout your degree programme you may be awarded an MSc with Distinction. Requires final mark and dissertation mark of at least 70% and an average of at least 65% in taught modules. A student producing consistently good work can obtain an award of MSc with Merit. Requires final mark and dissertation mark of at least 60% and an average of at least 55% in taught modules Your final mark is weighted at 50% taught stage and 50% dissertation stage for one year Masters For students on Programmes with placement your final mark is weighted 45% taught stage, 10% placement stage and 45% dissertation stage.

CC BY SA 2.0 by Marion Doss

To succeed at Master’s level requires a lot of dedication and hard-work. A full-time student should expect to work 40 hours a week on average but work-load is not evenly spread and you are likely to find yourself busier at the end of semesters. It is therefore important to manage your time well and be aware of your commitments and deadlines. You will be provided with a coursework timetable to help you with this.
